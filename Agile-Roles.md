### **1. What are the key roles in an Agile team, and what does each role do?**

#### **Developers**
Developers build the product. They write code, fix bugs, review pull requests, and deliver features based on user stories. They work closely with the team to create working software.

#### **QA (Quality Assurance)**
QA ensures the quality of the product. They design tests, find bugs, confirm functionality, and help maintain high standards. Their goal is to make sure the user experience is smooth.

#### **Product Manager (PM)**
The PM defines what needs to be built and why. They prioritize features, understand user needs, and maintain the product vision. They also help the team understand requirements clearly.

#### **Agile Project Manager (Agile PM)**
The Agile PM helps the team work efficiently. They remove blockers, support planning, and ensure the team follows Agile practices. Their goal is to support productivity and communication.

#### **Other Supporting Roles (Stakeholders, UX, Customer Support)**
These roles help the team by giving feedback, reporting issues, and improving the overall user experience.  
- UX designers improve usability  
- Customer support shares user problems  
- Stakeholders guide business needs  

---

### **2. How do these roles collaborate in Kanban vs. Scrum?**

#### **Scrum**
- Collaboration happens inside sprints  
- Developers, QA, and PM work according to sprint goals  
- Daily standups, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives  
- Clear rhythm and structured team ceremonies  

#### **Kanban**
- Work flows continuously with no sprints  
- Roles collaborate anytime when a task moves across the board  
- QA and Dev work closely to finish tasks quickly  
- PM updates priorities on the board when needed  
- Less ceremony, more flexibility
