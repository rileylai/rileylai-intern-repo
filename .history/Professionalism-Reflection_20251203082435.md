### **1. What are examples of professional vs. unprofessional behaviour in a casual work environment?**

**Professional behaviour:**
- Communicating clearly and respectfully  
- Being on time for meetings  
- Following up on action items  
- Asking questions when something is unclear  
- Giving feedback politely  
- Keeping commitments and updating the team if delays happen  

**Unprofessional behaviour:**
- Ignoring messages or being unresponsive  
- Speaking rudely or dismissively  
- Missing deadlines without explanation  
- Blaming others instead of solving problems  
- Showing frustration or anger in messages  
- Not taking responsibility for mistakes  

A casual culture still requires responsibility and respect.

### **2. What does respectful communication look like in remote teams?**

Respectful communication means:
- Using a friendly and calm tone  
- Keeping messages short and clear  
- Asking clarifying questions instead of assuming  
- Responding within a reasonable time frame  
- Saying ‚Äúthank you‚Äù and appreciating others  
- Being patient with delays due to time zones  

Tone matters even more in remote work because people cannot see facial expressions.

---

### **3. How can you give and receive feedback professionally?**

To give feedback:
- Focus on actions, not the person  
- Use clear examples  
- Speak kindly and directly  
- Assume good intent  
- Offer suggestions for improvement  

To receive feedback:
- Listen fully before responding  
- Avoid getting defensive  
- Ask questions to understand  
- Thank the person for the feedback  
- Take action to improve  

Feedback should help the team grow.

### **5. What steps can you take to make sure your communication is clear, respectful, and inclusive?**

I can:
- Use simple and direct language  
- Avoid long messages with too many ideas  
- Check my tone before sending  
- Ask if someone needs clarification  
- Respect different time zones  
- Confirm understanding when necessary  

Clear communication reduces misunderstandings.

### **6. How can you help create a positive, fun, and professional work environment?**

I can:
- Keep a friendly and calm attitude  
- Encourage teammates when they make progress  
- Show appreciation for their work  
- Follow through on commitments  
- Share updates regularly  
- Help others when they are stuck  

Small actions make a supportive work culture.

### **7. How can you prepare effectively for meetings with supervisors and colleagues?**

I can:
- Read the project brief or issue beforehand  
- Prepare questions in advance  
- Review my progress  
- Write down action items  
- Join the meeting on time  
- Share updates clearly and directly  

Preparation shows professionalism.

### **8. What would it look like to be proactive during your internship?**

Being proactive means:
- Asking questions early instead of staying stuck  
- Giving regular updates without being asked  
- Suggesting solutions instead of only reporting problems  
- Taking initiative to learn new skills  
- Trying to solve small issues independently first  

Proactive behaviour helps the team work smoothly.

---

### **9. How might you follow up with colleagues or supervisors if you need information or action from them?**

I would:
1. Wait for the expected response time for each communication channel (email, Discord, SMS, call).
2. Send a friendly follow-up message through the same communication method.
3. If there is still no response after a day, send a polite reminder.
4. If the matter becomes urgent, escalate using the recommended communication channel.
5. Maintain a respectful, patient tone throughout the process.

Follow-ups should be polite and not pushy.
