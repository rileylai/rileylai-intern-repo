### **1. What are common cyber security threats in a remote work environment?**

Common threats include:
- Phishing emails pretending to be official messages  
- Malware from insecure downloads  
- Password theft  
- Unsafe Wi-Fi networks  
- Social engineering attacks  

Remote work increases risks because people connect from home or public networks.

---

### **2. What are best practices for keeping your devices and accounts secure?**

Good practices include:
- Keeping software updated  
- Using strong and unique passwords  
- Enabling two-factor authentication  
- Avoiding unknown links or attachments  
- Using secure Wi-Fi with a password  
- Locking your device when not using it  

---

### **3. Why is it important to lock your computer when away from your desk?**

Locking your computer prevents anyone from seeing sensitive information or accessing your accounts.  
Even if you step away for a short time, leaving your device unlocked is a major security risk.

---

### **4. How should you handle phishing attempts and suspicious links?**

You should:
- Not click the link  
- Check the sender‚Äôs address  
- Look for spelling mistakes or unusual requests  
- Report the message  
- Delete it immediately  

If you are not sure, always verify with the official team.

---

### **5. What makes a strong password, and why should you use a password manager?**

A strong password:
- Has at least 12 characters  
- Includes numbers, symbols, upper and lower case letters  
- Is not related to personal information  

A password manager helps create and store strong passwords so you never reuse the same one.

---

### **6. Why is two-factor authentication (2FA) important, and when should you enable it?**

2FA adds an extra layer of security by requiring a code or device confirmation.  
Even if someone steals your password, they cannot log in without your second factor.

You should enable 2FA for:
- Email  
- Bank accounts  
- Work accounts  
- Cloud storage  
- Any important service
